## 6. Function Analysis

Given

$$
f(x) = 3x^2 - 12x + 7
$$

To find local extrema, take the derivative:

$$
f'(x) = 6x - 12
$$

Set it equal to zero:

$$
6x - 12 = 0
$$

$$
x = 2
$$

Now check the second derivative:

$$
f''(x) = 6
$$

Since $f''(x) > 0$, the function has a **local minimum** at $x=2$.

Find the function value:

$$
f(2) = 3(2)^2 - 12(2) + 7
= 12 - 24 + 7
= -5
$$

### Final answer

The function has a **local minimum** at

$$
(2,-5)
$$

There is **no local maximum**.
