## 9. Optimization Problem

A rectangle is under the curve

$$
y = 3 - x^2
$$

in the first quadrant.

Let the top-right corner of the rectangle be $(x,y)$ on the curve. Then

$$
y = 3 - x^2
$$

The area is

$$
A(x) = x(3 - x^2) = 3x - x^3
$$

Differentiate:

$$
A'(x) = 3 - 3x^2
$$

Set equal to zero:

$$
3 - 3x^2 = 0
$$

$$
x^2 = 1
$$

Since we are in the first quadrant,

$$
x = 1
$$

Then

$$
y = 3 - 1^2 = 2
$$

### Final answer

The rectangle with maximum area has dimensions

$$
1 \times 2
$$

So,

- width = $1$
- height = $2$
