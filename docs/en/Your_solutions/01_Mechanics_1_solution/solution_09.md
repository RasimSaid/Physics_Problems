# 🥊 9. Momentum Comparison: Speed vs. Mass

Momentum ($p$) is the "oomph" an object carries. It tells us how much effort is required to bring a moving object to a halt. It is the product of how heavy you are ($m$) and how fast you go ($v$).

---

### 🤺 The Contenders

We have a classic battle between a **light but fast** fly and a **heavy but slow** tennis ball.

| Object | Mass ($m$) | Velocity ($v$) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Common Fly** | $0.002 \text{ kg}$ ($2\text{g}$) | $10 \text{ m/s}$ ($36\text{ km/h}$) |
| **Tennis Ball** | $0.060 \text{ kg}$ ($60\text{g}$) | $1 \text{ m/s}$ ($3.6\text{ km/h}$) |



---

### 🧮 The Calculation: Finding the "Impact"

Even though the fly is moving **10 times faster** than the tennis ball, the ball is **30 times heavier**. Let's see how the math plays out:

* **Fly ($p_1$):**
    $$p_1 = 0.002 \text{ kg} \cdot 10 \text{ m/s} = \mathbf{0.02 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}}$$

* **Tennis Ball ($p_2$):**
    $$p_2 = 0.060 \text{ kg} \cdot 1 \text{ m/s} = \mathbf{0.06 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}}$$

---

### 🏆 The Verdict: Mass Wins This Round

Comparing the two results:
$$\mathbf{p_{ball} (0.06) > p_{fly} (0.02)}$$



**The Insight:** The tennis ball has **3 times more momentum** than the fly. If both were to hit a window, the tennis ball—even at its "walking pace"—would exert a much greater force upon impact than the buzzing fly. In this specific case, the ball's superior mass comfortably outweighs the fly's superior speed.

> **💡 Fun Fact:** To have the same momentum as this slow tennis ball, the 2-gram fly would need to increase its speed to **$30 \text{ m/s}$** ($108 \text{ km/h}$)!
